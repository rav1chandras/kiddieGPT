Built for trust

Made to help — not to cheat.

KiddieGPT isn't grade-safe because we say so. It's built so kids do the thinking, answers stay checked, and parents stay in control.

  • Answer-gating — the final answer stays hidden behind a Reveal button until the steps are done
  • A parent PIN locks answers so kids can't skip straight to the solution
  • Every math answer is solved twice and cross-checked before it's shown
  • Private by default — activity stays on the device; no student name or email stored
Frequently asked

Questions, answered.

Is this actually safe for my kid?

KiddieGPT is grade-safe by design. It teaches the steps instead of handing over answers, and a parent PIN can keep the final answer locked until the work is done. Content runs through safety moderation, there are no ads, and almost everything stays on your device.

What age is KiddieGPT designed for?

Students from early elementary through high school. A grade-band setting — K–2, 3–5, 6–8, or 9–12 — controls how KiddieGPT talks: vocabulary, sentence length, and how far the math goes. A younger child and an older one get very different help on the same page.

How is this different from giving my kid ChatGPT?

ChatGPT wasn't built for students — and it just hands over the answer. KiddieGPT is built to teach: it shows math one step at a time and keeps the answer gated, double-checks every solution, reads and explains the exact page or worksheet your child is on, and gives parents a PIN to lock answers.

Do I need an OpenAI account?

On the Free plan you bring your own OpenAI or Anthropic key (developer mode). Premium and Family include built-in KiddieGPT AI — no API key, no separate account, no surprise bills.

What data do you collect?

Almost everything stays on your device. Your child's questions, activity history, vocabulary list, and parent settings all live in Chrome's local storage — not on our servers. See our Privacy Policy for the full picture.
